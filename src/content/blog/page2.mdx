In the late 18<sup>th</sup> century, scientists started to use measurable qualities, like weight, to describe different elements. Antoine Lavoisier, a French chemist who is often called the father of modern chemistry, started to create a group of 33 "simple substances". Published in his *Traité Élémentaire de Chimie*, he described an element as a single substance that cannot be broken down further. He also discovered the law of the conservation of mass, wherein no substance is ever destroyed or created, it is just transformed.
