# Discovery
In 1913, English chemist Henry Moseley used x-rays to measure the wavelengths of elements and correlated these measurements as the atomic number of elements. After re-arranging the periodic table based on number instead of mass, he found that many disparities from earlier tables had dissappeared.

# The Modern Periodic Table
In the modern table, the horizontal rows are called periods while the vertical columns are called groups. Metals are on the extreme left, while non-metals are on the right. The groups contain elements with similar chemical properties. Just by looking at the periodic table, one can tell the atomic structure of elements and the chemical similarities or dissimilarities between them.
