John Dalton, a schoolteacher from Manchester, was the first to suggest classifying elements based on the weights of their atoms. This crucial step of sorting by atomic weight would allow for the creation of periodic tables. He also discovered that all matter is made of atoms as well as atoms of one element are alike but their atoms differ from those of other elements. Dalton was also the first to use ball-and-stick models to describe elements.
