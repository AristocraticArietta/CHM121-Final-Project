# Breakthrough

During a conference in Karlsruhe, Germany in 1860, a list of all the known elements and their atomic masses was published. They determined that hydrogen had an atomic weight of 1 and all other elements would be compared to hydrogen. For example, with carbon being 12 times heavier than hydrogen, carbon would have an atomic weight of 12.

# The Law of Octaves

John Newlands created the first periodic table with the order determined by increasing atomic masses. He also created the law of octaves, wherein every eight elements had similar properties and these properties would repeat every eight elements. However, he did not leave any room for undiscovered elements.
