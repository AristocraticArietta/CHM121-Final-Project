The earliest ideas about the elements came from Ancient Greek philosophers. Their ideas were based on essential qualities, or elements, or varying materials. The four elements they decided on were air (hot and wet), water (cold and wet), earth (cold or dry), and fire (dry and hot). 

Over time, as new substances were discovered, apothecaries and alchemists tried to group different substances together, but there was no agreeance on order or groupings.
